What is Quartz?

Engineered quartz slabs are a man-made material used for kitchen countertops and design. They are composed of natural quartz, one of the hardest minerals found in the earth, mixed with resin binders and pigments. The result is a durable and low-maintenance surface that is resistant to scratches, heat, and stains. Engineered quartz slabs come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, and can mimic the look of natural stone like granite, marble, or limestone. They are also non-porous, making them easy to clean and sanitize. They are becoming increasingly popular for kitchen design because they are a cost-effective and low-maintenance alternative to natural stone countertops.

What is Granite?

Granite is a natural stone that is widely used for kitchen countertops and design. It is a coarse-grained, igneous rock composed mainly of feldspar, quartz, and mica. Granite is known for its durability, heat resistance, and resistance to scratches and stains. It is also highly valued for its natural beauty, with a wide range of colors and patterns available. Granite countertops are a popular choice for kitchen design because they add a touch of elegance and luxury to any space. They are also easy to maintain and can last for many years with proper care.

Can I use Marble for my kitchen countertops?

Marble can be used for kitchen counters, but it does have some limitations in terms of staining, heat, and scratching.
